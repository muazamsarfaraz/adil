## 1. Association of Muslim Lawyers (AML)

### Organisation Details
- **Founded:** 1993 (officially launched 1995)
- **Type:** Voluntary organisation
- **Status:** Active
- **Employees:** ~9 (per RocketReach)
- **LinkedIn Followers:** 2,717
- **Mission:** Campaigns for legal rights for Muslims and others, and to aid Muslims working in the legal professions. Focus on advocacy, mentoring, networking, seminars, and interfaith/inter-community work.
- **Description:** Works on issues of importance and significance to Muslims in the UK as well as highlighting issues that affect Muslims internationally.

## 2. Muslim Lawyers Action Group (MLAG)

### Organisation Details
- **Type:** Professional network / not-for-profit
- **Status:** Active (note: a Companies House entity "MUSLIM LAWYERS ACTION GROUP LTD" (Company No. 12675227) was incorporated 16 June 2020 and dissolved 16 March 2021, registered at 53 Montrose Avenue, Luton, LU3 1HP. The organisation continues to operate independently of this dissolved entity.)
- **Affiliation:** Recognised by the Inner Temple (one of the four Inns of Court) under its Equality, Diversity and Inclusivity programme.
- **Description:** The largest active network of Muslim solicitors, barristers, trainee solicitors, pupil barristers, paralegals, academics and students.
- **Mission:** Committed to developing an ecosystem of lawyers and legal professionals who promote excellence, justice and the respect for human rights. Offers a platform, training and events for Muslim lawyers to connect, network, exchange ideas, support each other, and work on projects together.

### Contact Details
- **Website:** https://mlag.legal (NOTE: site was inaccessible via automated fetch at time of research; may require browser access)
- **Who We Are page:** https://www.mlag.legal/who-we-are
- **Contact page:** https://www.mlag.legal/projects
- **Instagram:** @muslimlawyersactiongroup (https://www.instagram.com/muslimlawyersactiongroup/)
- **Inner Temple page:** https://www.innertemple.org.uk/who-we-are/how-we-operate/equality-diversity-and-inclusivity/muslim-lawyers-action-group/

### Key People
- **Shaheen Mamun** - Muslim solicitor, serves on the Law Society's EDI committee, authored Law Gazette article on advancing Muslim lawyers' role, co-founder of Black Antelope Law, closely associated with MLAG initiatives.
- **Ibrahim Mohammed** - Multi-award-winning Education Influencer and Future Trainee Solicitor. BA from Cambridge, MSc from Oxford. Interest in human rights law. Over 130,000+ YouTube subscribers. Works as intern at MLAG.
- **Maryam Shah** - BA Jurisprudence (Oxford), MSc Law (LSE). Interested in Discrimination and Equality law. Spoke at 10 Downing Street on BAME attainment gap. Research intern for MLAG's Public Interest Litigation team and EDI team.

### Teams / Departments
- Public Interest Litigation team
- Equality, Diversity and Inclusion team

### Member Directory
- **No public member directory found.** MLAG does not appear to maintain a publicly searchable list of members.

### How to Join / Access Network
- **Membership page:** https://mlag.legal/become-a-member
- **Membership benefits include:**
  - Up-to-date news about MLAG's work
  - Opportunities to assist with projects
  - Invitations to MLAG events
  - Participation in CPD events
  - Access to a WhatsApp group with legal professionals or students
  - Free or discounted entry to MLAG conferences, seminars and workshops
  - Exclusive social and wellbeing events
  - Invitations to networking events
- Specific membership fees were not publicly listed in search results. Contact via website for details.
- MLAG supports members aspiring to become judges, solicitors and barristers.

### Notable Information
- MLAG has interns supported by the **Aziz Foundation** (https://www.azizfoundation.org.uk/)
- Statistics cited by associated articles: Muslim solicitors increased from 5% (2019) to 6% of the profession. Approximately 361 barristers have disclosed Muslim identity. 3.5% of CILEX members identify as Muslim.

## 3. Muslim Lawyers' Hub

### Organisation Details
- **Founded:** August 2021
- **Founder:** Farzana Abdullah (ULaw graduate, former Clyde & Co trainee)
- **Type:** Community organisation / platform (hosted on WordPress)
- **Status:** Active
- **Community size:** 6,000+ members across Instagram and LinkedIn (as of 2023 reporting; 4,900+ in early 2023, growing since)
- **Newsletter subscribers:** 132+ (WordPress followers)
- **Mission:** Empowering Muslim students in their journey to becoming legal professionals. Aims to increase the number of Muslim lawyers in the UK through mentoring, events, advice and work experience.

### Contact Details
- **Website:** https://muslimlawyershub.com/
- **Email:** muslimlawyershub@gmail.com
- **Instagram:** @muslimlawyershub (https://www.instagram.com/muslimlawyershub/)
- **LinkedIn:** https://uk.linkedin.com/company/muslim-lawyers-hub
- **Threads:** https://www.threads.com/@muslimlawyershub
- **Founder LinkedIn:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/farzana-abdullah-645263179/

### About the Founder
- **Farzana Abdullah** was a third-year law student at ULaw and a future Clyde & Co trainee when she created the hub.
- Motivation: "I have been told on many occasions that I am unlikely to have a successful career in law due to my background." Struggled to find answers to questions about wearing an abaya in the workplace, Islamophobia, and navigating spaces with alcohol.
- Has partnered with high-profile law firms for workshops, insight events and networking.

### Member Directory
- **No public member directory.** The Hub is primarily a content, mentoring and networking platform rather than a directory service.

### How to Join / Access Network
- Follow on Instagram (@muslimlawyershub) and LinkedIn
- Subscribe to newsletter at https://muslimlawyershub.com/
- Email: muslimlawyershub@gmail.com
- The Hub partners with law firms to host workshops, insight events and networking opportunities.

### Helpful Organisations Listed by the Hub
The Hub maintains a useful list of related organisations at https://muslimlawyershub.com/organisations/ including:
- Aspiring Solicitors
- Bright Network
- Legal Cheek
- Forage
- Rare Recruitment
- Vantage
- Bridging the Bar
- Chambers Student

### Key Statistics (from Law Gazette article by Farzana Abdullah, May 2023)
- Only 5% of UK lawyers are Muslim (no change since 2019 at time of writing; later updated to 6% by Shaheen Mamun)
- Four main barriers for Muslim lawyers: religious accommodation, networking & mentorship, representation, and education

## 5. Summary: Key Gaps & Observations

### No Public Member Directories Found
None of the three primary organisations (AML, MLAG, Muslim Lawyers' Hub) maintain a publicly accessible directory of Muslim lawyers. The networks function primarily through:
- Events and networking
- Social media communities (LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook groups)
- WhatsApp groups (MLAG members)
- Mentoring programmes

### Potential Data Sources for Finding Muslim Lawyers
1. **SRA (Solicitors Regulation Authority) register** - searchable but does not filter by religion
2. **Bar Council records** - ~361 barristers have disclosed Muslim identity
3. **CILEX** - 3.5% of members identify as Muslim
4. **Law Society** - diversity data collected but not published as a searchable directory
5. **LinkedIn** - searching within AML and MLAG follower networks
6. **Facebook groups** - AML's public Facebook group
7. **SolicitorConnect** - has an Islamic/Sharia law solicitor category
8. **Muslim Lawyer UK / Muslim Solicitors** - referral service with vetted lawyers

### Website Accessibility Issues (as of 2026-03-25)
- aml.org.uk - intermittently inaccessible
- mlag.legal - connection refused via automated tools (may work in browser)
- aml.org.gridhosted.co.uk - inaccessible

### Key Statistic
Muslim solicitors represent approximately 6% of all solicitors in England and Wales, up from 5% in 2019. Approximately 361 barristers have disclosed Muslim identity.
