## 1. SPECIALIST CHARITIES & ADVOCACY ORGANISATIONS

### Tell MAMA (Measuring Anti-Muslim Attacks)
- **Type:** Charity / Monitoring & Victim Support
- **Website:** https://tellmamauk.org
- **Phone:** 0800 456 1226 (freephone)
- **SMS:** 0115 707 0007
- **WhatsApp:** 0734 184 6086
- **Email:** info@tellmamauk.org
- **What they offer:** Casework, emotional support, legal signposting, advocacy, court attendance support, counselling, evidence collection, police reporting support. Over 60,000 British Muslims supported since inception. 13+ years operating.
- **Legal partners:** Mishcon de Reya LLP acts as Tell MAMA's legal representatives. Tell MAMA does not provide direct legal representation but signposts and refers to legal providers.
- **Coverage:** England & Wales (national)
- **Notable:** Government-funded. Publishes annual hate crime data reports. Key data source for policy makers.

### Islamophobia Response Unit (IRU)
- **Type:** Charity (Registered No. 1196838)
- **Website:** https://www.theiru.org.uk
- **Email:** info@theiru.org.uk
- **Contact page:** https://www.theiru.org.uk/contact/
- **What they offer:** Free legal guidance (advisory, not representation), emotional support, data collection, referrals to partner agencies. Volunteer caseworkers and legal supervisors.
- **Coverage:** England & Wales (UK residents only)
- **Key stats (from Jan 2026 annual report):** 377% surge in Islamophobia incidents 2023-2024. 906 incidents investigated 2021-2025. Discrimination = 37% of all cases. Average employment tribunal settlement: £43,234. Education cases: 18.6% of incidents.
- **Notable cases:** Discrimination by estate agents (housing), teacher bullying in classrooms, false classroom allegations leading to dismissal, Islamophobic bus incidents, school uniform/hijab cases.
- **LawWorks partnership:** Listed on LawWorks pro bono directory.
- **Limitation:** Does NOT provide formal legal representation or advocacy. Advisory and conciliatory approach only.

- **Type:** Non-profit advocacy organisation
- **Website:** https://www.mend.org.uk
- **Phone:** +44 208 980 4591
- **Email:** info@mend.org.uk
- **What they offer:** Media monitoring, political lobbying, community engagement, Islamophobia awareness seminars, media/political literacy masterclasses. Primarily a political engagement and advocacy body.
- **Legal support:** MEND does NOT provide direct legal support or representation. Focuses on policy advocacy and community empowerment.

- **Notable:** Based in Skelmersdale, Lancashire since 2021. Extensive grassroots community engagement programme.

### CAGE International
- **Type:** Advocacy organisation
- **Website:** https://www.cage.ngo
- **Address:** Premier Business Centre, 47-49 Park Royal Road, London NW10 7LQ
- **Helpline:** 0300 030 2243
- **Office:** 020 7377 6700
- **Email:** contact@cage.ngo
- **What they offer:** Free casework service. Informs Muslims of their rights, connects them with lawyers. Focuses on War on Terror-related state oppression, counter-terrorism policies, Prevent programme cases.

### Islamic Human Rights Commission (IHRC)
- **Type:** Charity / Advocacy (est. 1997)
- **Website:** https://www.ihrc.org.uk
- **Address:** PO Box 598, Wembley, HA9 7XH
- **What they offer:** First Muslim civil rights group in UK with standalone in-house legal department providing affordable legal services. Handles discrimination cases involving Islamophobia and anti-Muslim racism. UN consultative status since 2007.
- **Coverage:** UK-wide (with international work)
- **Notable cases:** University lecturer indirect racial discrimination victory (2014). Campaigns on Babar Ahmad/Talha Ahsan extradition cases.

### Muslim Women's Network UK (MWNUK)
- **Type:** Charity / Helpline
- **Website:** https://www.mwnuk.co.uk
- **Helpline:** 0303 330 0288 (free, multi-lingual, confidential)
- **Channels:** Phone, text, webchat, email
- **What they offer:** Culturally sensitive multilingual helpline, casework (including IDVA), legal rights information, referrals to legal providers and other services. Covers domestic abuse, mental health, discrimination, homelessness.
- **Coverage:** England & Wales (national)
- **Notable:** Launched Muslim Safety Net Service (2025) to capture unreported discrimination/hate incidents. Although primarily supporting Muslim women, open to women of any faith.

### Muslim Council of Britain (MCB)
- **Type:** National representative body
- **Website:** https://mcb.org.uk
- **What they offer:** Advocacy, policy engagement, formal complaints to EHRC. Filed formal Islamophobia complaints against Conservative Party with EHRC (evidence on 300+ individuals).
- **Coverage:** UK-wide

## 8. KEY LEGAL FRAMEWORK NOTES

- **Equality Act 2010:** Protects religion/belief as a protected characteristic. Covers direct/indirect discrimination, harassment, victimisation in employment, education, housing, and public services.
- **No specific anti-Islamophobia law** exists in UK. Anti-Muslim activity covered by general hate crime legislation.
- **Government working group** (chaired by Dominic Grieve KC) established to produce working definition of Anti-Muslim Hatred/Islamophobia.
- **Legal aid** available for discrimination cases through firms with Discrimination Legal Aid Contracts (e.g., Kesar & Co).
- **Civil Legal Advice** can provide legal aid referrals for discrimination cases.
- **Average employment tribunal settlement** for Islamophobia-related discrimination: £43,234 (IRU 2026 report).
- **Muslims account for 45%** of all religious hate crimes in England & Wales.
- **1,000+ Islamophobic hate crimes** recorded in London in 2025; estimated 80% go unreported.

## 9. RECOMMENDED FIRST STEPS FOR VICTIMS

1. **Immediate danger:** Call 999
2. **Report the incident:** Tell MAMA (0800 456 1226) or IRU (info@theiru.org.uk)
3. **Report to police:** Call 101 (non-emergency)
4. **Get free legal guidance:** Contact IRU or IHRC Legal
5. **Check legal aid eligibility:** Use gov.uk legal aid checker or contact Kesar & Co
6. **Find a solicitor:** Use Law Society Find a Solicitor tool, LawWorks, or contact firms listed above
7. **For employment discrimination:** Contact ACAS (0300 123 1100) before tribunal claim
8. **For housing discrimination:** Contact Shelter (0808 800 4444) + Hodge Jones & Allen
9. **For education discrimination:** Contact IRU + consider Mishcon de Reya for uniform/hijab cases
